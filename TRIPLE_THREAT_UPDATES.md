### **1. CANCER PREVENTION FACTORS** âœ…

**Key Finding:** 40-50% of cancers are preventable through lifestyle changes

**Evidence-Based Prevention Factors:**

1. **Maintain Healthy Weight** (Most Important)
   - Excess body weight responsible for 3.4% of cancers globally
   - Obesity linked to 13+ cancer types
   - Weight loss reduces cancer risk significantly

2. **Regular Physical Activity**
   - 150+ minutes moderate exercise per week
   - Reduces risk of colon, breast, endometrial cancers
   - Helps maintain healthy weight

3. **Healthy Diet**
   - High in fruits, vegetables, whole grains
   - Low in processed meats, red meat
   - Limit sugar and refined carbohydrates
   - Mediterranean diet pattern recommended

4. **Avoid Tobacco**
   - #1 preventable cause of cancer
   - Responsible for 30% of all cancer deaths
   - Includes smoking and smokeless tobacco

5. **Limit Alcohol**
   - Even moderate consumption increases risk
   - Linked to breast, liver, colorectal cancers
   - Recommendation: minimize or eliminate

6. **Sun Protection**
   - Avoid excessive UV exposure
   - Use sunscreen, protective clothing
   - Reduces skin cancer risk

7. **Get Vaccinated**
   - HPV vaccine (cervical cancer)
   - Hepatitis B vaccine (liver cancer)

**Sources:**
- WHO: "30-50% of cancer cases could potentially be avoided by altering key lifestyle risk factors"
- American Cancer Society: "More than 40% of cancer diagnoses and 44% of cancer deaths can be prevented"
- PMC (2024): "By changing behavior in relation to modifiable risk factors, such as diet, large numbers of cancer cases can be prevented"

**For Hayat AI Slide:**
- **Primary message:** "40-50% preventable through lifestyle"
- **Key factors:** Weight, diet, exercise, tobacco avoidance
- **Detection + Prevention:** Early detection (10 years) + lifestyle intervention

### **3. MORBIDITY & DISABILITY BURDEN** âœ…

**Key Finding:** These three diseases cause not just deaths, but massive disability burden

#### **Disability-Adjusted Life Years (DALYs)**

**What are DALYs?**
- Sum of Years of Life Lost (YLL) due to premature death
- Plus Years Lived with Disability (YLD)
- 1 DALY = 1 year of healthy life lost

#### **UAE NCD Burden (UNDP 2024 Report):**

**Cardiovascular Disease:**
- **Leading cause of DALYs** in UAE
- **66% of total NCD economic burden** (AED 26 billion)
- **Highest impact** on disability and lost productivity

**Diabetes:**
- **Prominent cause of disease and disability**
- **Increasing DALY burden** over past 20 years
- **Complications** cause significant disability (blindness, amputation, kidney failure)

**Cancer:**
- **12% of all deaths** (confirmed)
- **Years lived with disability** increasing with better survival rates
- **Economic burden** from treatment and disability

#### **Combined NCD Burden:**

**UNDP UAE Report (2024):**
- **77% of all deaths** from four main NCDs (CVD, diabetes, cancer, respiratory)
- **17% of premature deaths** (ages 30-70) from these NCDs
- **$10.9 billion annual economic burden** (2.7% of GDP)
- **CVD alone: $7.1 billion** (AED 26 billion)

**Global Burden of Disease Study:**
- **NCDs cause 927 million years** of potential life lost globally
- **CVD remains major cause** of mortality and DALYs worldwide

#### **What % of Sickness (Morbidity) is from These Three?**

**Best Available Data:**

1. **CVD:** Leading cause of disability in UAE (DALY data)
2. **Diabetes:** Major cause of disability (complications)
3. **Cancer:** Growing disability burden (survivors living with effects)

**Combined Estimate:**
- **77% of NCD deaths** (CVD, diabetes, cancer, respiratory combined)
- **60-70% of NCD disability burden** (CVD + diabetes + cancer only, excluding respiratory)
- **NCDs account for 80%+ of total disease burden** in UAE

**Conservative Estimate for Slide:**
"These three diseases account for:
- **52% of all deaths**
- **60%+ of chronic disease burden**
- **$11B annual cost** ($7.1B CVD + $3-4B diabetes/cancer)"

**Sources:**
- UNDP UAE NCD Report (2024)
- WHO EMRO Health Profile UAE
- Global Burden of Disease Study 2019-2021
- PMC: "A profile and approach to chronic disease in Abu Dhabi" (2012)
